# Designer disciplines (bankless)

Replaces the ~100-entry `designer-bank.md`. Instead of ms-sampling one practitioner from a curated per-discipline list, the model **generates 2 candidates per discipline live** (10 disciplines × 2 = 20-pool) conditioned on the locked framing, honoring the per-discipline reach-into roster + the graphic-design ban-list + the reach-beyond mandate, then ms-samples 1 from the 20.

What was load-bearing in the old bank was **not** its 100 names. It was: (1) the 10-discipline structure, (2) the graphic-design Swiss/Dutch anti-canon exclusion, (3) the geographic / temporal / gender breadth mandate. Those survive here as rules. The names below are **reach-into territory pointers** (like typography's non-canon source roster), not a closed pool — generate freely within and beyond them, especially toward practitioners the examples don't name.

## The 10 disciplines (stable — do not invent others)

graphic design / type design / industrial design / architecture / fine art / photography / fashion / film + cinematic / digital + UX + interactive / craft + vernacular + regional.

## Two candidates per discipline (→ 20-pool)

For each discipline, generate **2 real, specific practitioners** (name + era + a citable signature work — never a description), conditioned on the locked framing:

- **Slot A — framing-fit:** the strongest fit whose voice carries the framing. May be well-known IF genuinely apt.
- **Slot B — non-canon counter-weight:** an under-represented / non-Western / contemporary-working practitioner drawn from the reach-into roster. This slot is the structural counter-weight to the model's canon-reaching reflex (the job the old bank slot did).

Tag each candidate `discipline` + `slot` (`fit` | `noncanon`).

## Ban-list (graphic design — keep verbatim, load-bearing)

Do NOT generate the Swiss/Dutch modernist graphic-design canon for the graphic-design candidates (either slot): **Karel Martens, Wim Crouwel, Massimo Vignelli, Josef Müller-Brockmann, Irma Boom, Stefan Sagmeister, David Carson, Herb Lubalin, Jan Tschichold, Herbert Bayer, Richard Lohse, Wolfgang Weingart, Catalogtree, Mevis & Van Deursen, Experimental Jetset, Studio Dumbar.** Their absence from graphic design is the load-bearing decision — they are already over-represented in the model's reach.

Also avoid: generic "world-class designer" placeholders (each candidate must bring a *specific* discipline voice); living celebrity branding designers whose voice is too pre-defined (Pentagram partners, Wieden+Kennedy creatives).

## Reach-beyond mandate

Over-represent non-Western, contemporary-working, and women practitioners relative to design-history-textbook coverage — the same breadth the curated bank enforced. A 20-pool that is mostly mid-century Western men is a generation failure; regenerate.
